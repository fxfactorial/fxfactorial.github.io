ReactNative

We all love ReactNative but for those of us that have worked in the ecosystem, it can be challenging in tedious ways.

The ecosystem moves extremely fast, a new version of ReactNative is released every month

Hence you basically need to be roughly ~4 months at most from the newest version for the least painful version upgrades

ReactNative also does not provide many APIs that you probably need to make a full featured app, like Maps.

Expo

This is where Expo comes in, Expo provides a stable API surface for you to use

Practically what does this mean? It means that you spend your time actually making your application, your business logic rather than yak shaving various busted dependencies.

Expo also make it ridiculously easy to integrate with all the important APIs, like Google Auth sign in, Facebook auth signin, and push notifications, adding fonts, Amplitude app analytics, WebGL, FileSystem and much more

An expo app also has some killer features that you never need to setup, like code push. It just works.

Pure JavaScript

When you make an expo app, you only write JavaScript. This means that with expo you can make iOS apps even on Windows, you do not need to have a Macbook

Your application is built on our build servers, this means you never have to open XCode or Android Studio.
